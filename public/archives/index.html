Common statistical tests are linear models

 Jul 7, 2019
The book Common statistical tests are linear models (or: how to teach stats), by Jonas Lindeløv, beautifully demonstrates how many common statistical tests (such as the t-test, ANOVA and chi-squared) are special cases of the linear model. It also demonstrates that many non-parametric tests, which are needed when certain test assumptions do not hold, can be approximated by linear models using the rank of values. To better understand this I wrote my own version of the book, Common statistical tests are linear models (a work through), which builds on the original.

Funding cuts and deprivation in England

 Apr 4, 2018
The most deprived parts of England have borne the brunt of public spending cuts since 2010. As spending now begins to increase, this could bring relief to some of the hardest-hit local councils. However, this analysis shows that the gains - unlike the cuts - will be shared fairly evenly among both affluent and deprived parts of England. In 2010 the UK government embarked on a series of major spending cuts to reduce its budget deficit, in response to the financial crisis.
