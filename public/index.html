A dot-density map of Dallas

 May 5, 2018
As a new arrival in Dallas, it’s taken me a while to get to grips with its layout. Its sheer size and sprawl make it difficult to identify where Dallas ends and neighboring cities begin. Expanses of new developments and construction appear to have blurred the lines between what were once distinct towns and cities. With this in mind, this post has two aims: first, to better understand the geography of Dallas, and second, to do so while exploring some of the GIS tools available in R, the open-source programming language.

However, this analysis shows that the gains - unlike the cuts - will be shared fairly evenly among both affluent and deprived parts of England.
